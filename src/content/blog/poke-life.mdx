There's a quote that changed the way I think about everything:

> "Life can be much broader once you discover one simple fact: everything around you that you call life was made up by people that were no smarter than you. And you can change it, you can influence it, you can build your own things that other people can use."

These words carry a weight that most motivational content doesn't. They're not telling you to believe in yourself in some abstract way. They're making a factual, verifiable claim.

## The World Was Built by People

Look around you. The buildings, the systems, the institutions, the apps, the norms — all of it was created by humans. Not gods. Not geniuses operating on a different plane. People. People with doubts, bad days, imperfect information, and limited time.

The same kind of people as you.

Once you really internalize this, it does something strange to your perception of the world. The fixed starts to look malleable. The permanent starts to look temporary. The impossible starts to look like it just hasn't been done yet.

## Poke It

The metaphor is simple: poke life, and something will pop out the other side.

Most people never poke. They navigate around the world as it is, treating its current configuration as given — as if the rules were handed down from somewhere immovable. They optimize within constraints they've never tested.

But constraints are mostly fiction. Rules are mostly conventions. Systems are mostly defaults that no one has bothered to change.

When you poke — when you question, build, challenge, create — you discover that the world is far more responsive than it looked from the outside.

## The Responsibility

Understanding this creates a responsibility.

If you can influence things and you don't, that's a choice. If you see something broken and you have the capacity to fix it — and most of us do, in some domain — then walking past it is a decision you're making.

The world is kind of messed up in a lot of ways. Most of those ways persist not because they're unsolvable but because not enough people have decided to push back.

## The Broader Life

Life can be much broader. That's the whole point. Not broader in terms of luxury or status, but broader in terms of what feels possible, what feels within reach, what feels like it belongs to you.

Most people live in a narrow version of their life — the version bounded by what they were told was available to them, what their environment modeled, what seemed realistic.

Poke it. The world is softer than it looks.
